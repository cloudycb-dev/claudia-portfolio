Scholarship & Research

The role of scholarly research in educational diagnostics and how evidence-based practices guide assessment, intervention, and instructional planning.

The Importance of Research in Educational Diagnostics

Educational Diagnosticians must be knowledgeable consumers of research. Understanding current scholarship supports:

  • Selection of valid and reliable assessment instruments
  • Use of evidence-based academic and behavioral interventions
  • Development of legally defensible Full Individual Evaluations (FIEs)
  • Accurate interpretation of evaluation data
  • Collaboration with educators and specialists

Research literacy ensures that students are evaluated and served using the most effective and current educational practices.

Evidence-Based Practice

Evidence-based practice (EBP) refers to instructional and intervention approaches demonstrated by high-quality research to be effective. Diagnosticians must understand which interventions have empirical support and how to interpret research findings accurately.

Examples within educational diagnosis include:

  • Standardized norm-referenced assessments
  • Curriculum-based measurement (CBM)
  • Explicit instruction
  • Positive behavior supports (PBS)
  • Progress-monitoring frameworks

Research Application to Evaluation and Instruction

Diagnosticians apply research findings to both evaluation and instructional decision-making. This includes:

  • Analyzing academic interventions to determine effectiveness
  • Interpreting assessment scores within research-based frameworks
  • Selecting reliable tools aligned with referral concerns
  • Recommending evidence-based instructional practices
  • Using research to support ARD/IEP committee decisions
