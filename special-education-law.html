Special Education Law & Procedures

An overview of federal and state laws governing special education eligibility, services, procedural safeguards, and ARD processes.

Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act

Section 504 is a federal civil rights law that ensures students with disabilities have equal access to education. A student qualifies for a 504 Plan if they have a physical or mental impairment that substantially limits one or more major life activities. A 504 Plan provides accommodations—not specialized instruction—to allow access to the general education curriculum.

Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA)

IDEA guarantees a Free Appropriate Public Education (FAPE) to eligible students with disabilities. It includes:

  • Requirements for evaluation and eligibility
  • Development of an Individualized Education Program (IEP)
  • Procedural safeguards for families
  • Special education and related services tailored to individual needs

IDEA ensures that students receive specially designed instruction, progress monitoring, and legally protected support.

Free Appropriate Public Education (FAPE)

FAPE means educational services must be individualized, data-driven, and designed to provide meaningful educational benefit. The U.S. Supreme Court requires that IEPs be “reasonably calculated to enable a child to make progress appropriate in light of the child’s circumstances” (Endrew F., 2017).

Least Restrictive Environment (LRE)

LRE requires districts to educate students with disabilities alongside nondisabled peers to the maximum extent appropriate. Removal to a specialized setting occurs only when the nature or severity of the disability prevents meaningful progress in the general education classroom—even with supports.

Child Find Obligations

School districts must identify, locate, and evaluate all children who may have disabilities and need special education services. Child Find applies to:

  • Students enrolled in public school
  • Highly mobile students
  • Private and homeschooled students
  • Students experiencing homelessness or foster care

Prompt referrals and timely evaluations are critical responsibilities of the district and educational diagnosticians.

Procedural Safeguards

Procedural safeguards protect the rights of students with disabilities and their parents. These include:

  • Prior written notice
  • Informed consent
  • Access to educational records
  • Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE)
  • Mediation and due process hearings

Region 18 ESC provides parent-friendly procedural safeguard documents, training, and resources to support districts.

ARD Committee Process

Texas uses the term Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) committee for the team that develops and reviews the IEP. The ARD committee includes:

  • Parent or guardian
  • General education teacher
  • Special education teacher
  • Campus or district representative
  • Evaluation specialist or diagnostician
  • Related service providers (as needed)

A well-run ARD meeting is collaborative, respectful, and grounded in data. The committee ensures that the IEP reflects student needs, strengths, and services required for success.
