Academic Methodology

Evidence-based instructional practices, RTI supports, and academic intervention strategies used to promote student success in reading, math, and writing.

Response to Intervention (RTI)

RTI is a multi-tiered framework used to identify and support students with academic difficulties. RTI emphasizes early intervention, data-driven instruction, and ongoing progress monitoring.

  • Tier 1: Core instruction delivered to all students
  • Tier 2: Targeted small-group interventions
  • Tier 3: Intensive individualized interventions

Diagnosticians review RTI data as part of the evaluation process to determine whether a student has received appropriate instruction before a referral for special education.

Evidence-Based Reading Practices

Effective reading instruction integrates the five essential components of literacy:

  • Phonemic awareness
  • Phonics
  • Fluency
  • Vocabulary
  • Reading comprehension

Common evidence-based strategies include:

  • Explicit instruction in phonics and decoding
  • Guided oral reading for fluency
  • Vocabulary development through semantic mapping
  • Comprehension strategy instruction (predicting, summarizing, questioning)

Evidence-Based Math Practices

Students benefit from explicit, systematic math instruction paired with conceptual understanding and real-world application.

  • Concrete–Representational–Abstract (CRA) framework
  • Explicit modeling with guided practice
  • Visual representations
  • Opportunities for frequent practice and feedback
  • Building problem-solving strategies

Evidence-Based Writing Practices

High-quality writing instruction includes modeling, scaffolding, and explicit teaching of writing processes.

  • Planning and organizing ideas (graphic organizers)
  • Sentence writing and expansion strategies
  • Explicit instruction in revising and editing
  • Use of mentor texts
  • Self-monitoring and checklists

Progress Monitoring

Progress monitoring is essential for evaluating whether interventions are effective. Tools such as curriculum-based measures (CBMs) allow teachers to track student growth and adjust instruction.

Effective progress monitoring includes:

  • Frequent data collection
  • Graphing student performance over time
  • Adjusting interventions based on data
  • Communicating progress with families
