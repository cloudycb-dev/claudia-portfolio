Clinical Assessment

An overview of MTSS processes, exclusionary factors, evaluation requirements, and components of a Full Individual Evaluation (FIE).

Multi-Tiered Systems of Support (MTSS)

MTSS is a framework designed to provide early intervention and targeted support for students experiencing academic or behavioral difficulties. Before referring a student for special education, districts must demonstrate that the student has received evidence-based interventions with documented progress monitoring.

MTSS typically includes three tiers:

  • Tier 1: High-quality core instruction for all students
  • Tier 2: Targeted small-group interventions
  • Tier 3: Intensive, individualized interventions

Documentation of MTSS efforts is required in Texas for SLD eligibility and is considered best practice for other eligibility categories as well.

Referral and Evaluation Process

If a student continues to struggle despite Tier 2 and Tier 3 interventions, the district may initiate a special education referral. Parental consent is required before evaluation can begin.

Following consent, the evaluation must:

  • Be completed within state timelines (45 school days in Texas)
  • Include multiple sources of data
  • Use valid, reliable, and standardized tools
  • Be conducted by trained professionals
  • Address all areas of suspected disability

Exclusionary Factors

Federal and state law require that learning difficulties must not be primarily the result of any of the following exclusionary factors:

  • Visual, hearing, or motor disabilities
  • Intellectual disability
  • Emotional disturbance
  • Cultural or environmental factors
  • Lack of appropriate instruction
  • Limited English proficiency

Evaluators must show that the student's difficulties cannot be explained by these factors before identifying a disability such as SLD or OHI.

Full Individual Evaluation (FIE)

The Full Individual Evaluation summarizes comprehensive data collected to determine eligibility for special education. A strong FIE includes:

  • Reason for referral and background information
  • Review of MTSS and intervention data
  • Classroom observations
  • Interviews with parents and teachers
  • Standardized cognitive and academic assessments
  • Speech, behavior, or occupational/physical therapy assessments (as needed)
  • Adaptive behavior measures
  • Analysis of exclusionary factors
  • Eligibility determinations
  • Recommendations for instruction and services

Evaluations must use the pattern of strengths and weaknesses (PSW) model or response to intervention (RTI) data for SLD determination in Texas.

Cognitive, Academic, and Behavioral Assessments

Evaluators select instruments based on student need and referral concerns. Common assessments include:

  • Cognitive: WISC-V, DAS-II, KABC-II
  • Academic: WIAT-4, WJ IV, KTEA-3
  • Behavior: BASC-3, Conners-3, Vineland-3

Tools must be used ethically, interpreted accurately, and administered in the student’s dominant language unless inappropriate to do so.
