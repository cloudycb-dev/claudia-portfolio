Clinical Assessment

Understanding MTSS, exclusionary factors, assessment practices, and how behavioral components inform comprehensive evaluation and diagnostic decision-making.

Multi-Tiered Systems of Support (MTSS)

MTSS is a schoolwide framework used to provide early intervention and support to students who are struggling academically or behaviorally. Before a student is considered for a special education referral, documentation of Tier 1, Tier 2, and Tier 3 interventions is required.

MTSS tiers include:

  • Tier 1: High-quality core instruction
  • Tier 2: Targeted small-group interventions
  • Tier 3: Intensive individualized interventions

MTSS data plays a critical role in eligibility decisions and ensures students have received scientifically based instruction before a referral.

Referral and Evaluation

When a student continues to experience difficulty despite interventions, a referral for a special education evaluation may be initiated. Once parental consent is obtained, the district must complete the evaluation within 45 school days (Texas timeline).

The evaluation must:

  • Use multiple sources of data
  • Be conducted by trained specialists
  • Include formal and informal measures
  • Assess all areas of suspected disability
  • Use tools that are valid, reliable, and nondiscriminatory

Exclusionary Factors

Diagnosticians must ensure difficulties are not primarily due to:

  • Lack of appropriate instruction
  • Limited English proficiency
  • Cultural, environmental, or economic factors
  • Emotional disturbance
  • Vision, hearing, or motor impairments
  • Intellectual disability

Evaluators must rule out exclusionary factors before determining eligibility for categories such as SLD and OHI.

Full Individual Evaluation (FIE)

The FIE summarizes all evaluation data and determines eligibility for special education services. A comprehensive FIE includes:

  • Reason for referral
  • Review of intervention data
  • Observations
  • Interviews with parents and teachers
  • Cognitive assessments
  • Academic achievement assessments
  • Behavior rating scales
  • Adaptive behavior assessments
  • Analysis of exclusionary factors
  • Eligibility findings
  • Recommendations for instruction

Assessment Tools

Common assessments used in clinical evaluation include:

  • Cognitive: WISC-V, KABC-II, DAS-II
  • Academic: WIAT-4, WJ-IV, KTEA-3
  • Behavior: BASC-3, Conners-3, Vineland-3

Tools must be selected based on referral concern and administered in the student’s dominant language whenever possible.
